The Federal Reserve raised interest rates by 25 basis points on Wednesday, marking the tenth consecutive hike since March 2022. Chair Jerome Powell signaled that further tightening remains on the table as inflation, while declining, remains above the 2 percent target. Equity markets fell sharply following the announcement, with the S&P 500 dropping 1.4 percent and the Nasdaq sliding 2.1 percent. Treasury yields climbed across the curve, pushing the 10-year yield to 4.85 percent. Analysts at Goldman Sachs warned that tighter credit conditions could weigh on corporate earnings through the second half of the year. Meanwhile, the unemployment rate held steady at 3.7 percent, complicating the Fed's dual mandate of price stability and maximum employment. Several regional banks reported declining net interest margins as deposit costs rose faster than loan yields. The dollar strengthened against a basket of currencies, pressuring emerging market assets. Oil prices dipped on demand concerns, with Brent crude settling at 87 dollars per barrel. Investors now await the next CPI print for clearer guidance on the trajectory of monetary policy.
